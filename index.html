About This Team

Bridging the Clinical Lab and the Bedside

At the Chin-Yee & Hsia Hematology Research Lab, we connect laboratory insights with real-world clinical decisions to transform care for patients with blood disorders.

Lab ↔ Clinic
  • Rare diseases focus: PNH, immune cytopenias, unexplained cytopenias
  • Biomarkers & AI: predictive models to sharpen decisions
  • Longitudinal datasets: linking lab signals to outcomes
  • Training: hands-on mentorship for students & fellows

“We bridge insights between the clinical laboratory and the bedside to transform patient care.”

— Lab Mission

Our Focus

Our research focuses on improving the early identification of rare hematologic diseases and studying how novel biomarkers and AI-driven tools can guide real-world clinical decision-making. We are also investigating how clonal evolution in conditions like paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria (PNH) shapes disease trajectories over time—linking longitudinal lab findings with patient outcomes.

Our People

Central to this work is better communication between laboratory and clinical teams, ensuring that new evidence, diagnostic reports, and biomarkers are shared clearly and translated into timely, evidence-based decisions. We are equally committed to training the next generation of physician-scientists in a collaborative environment where curiosity drives discovery—and discovery improves lives.
